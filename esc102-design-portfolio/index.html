Praxis And CTMFs

The framework behind the portfolio

Praxis

Frame, Diverge, Converge, Represent

Praxis is treated here as an iterative design cycle, not a linear checklist. Frame clarifies the situation, Diverge expands options, Converge selects under constraints, and Represent makes the work legible enough to test and communicate.

CTMF

Concepts, Tools, Models, and Frameworks

Each CTMF entry records one mechanism that actually shaped a decision. The focus is not just whether I used it, but whether it revealed something important, where it breaks down, and whether it fits or corrects my default design habits.

The pattern used throughout the site is consistent: CTMF -> Evidence -> Decision -> Reflection. That is the core unit of argument for this portfolio.

Coverage Overview

CTMF coverage across the four Praxis strands

Frame

Problem framing and constraints

Stakeholder framing, failure-mode decomposition, and invariant reduction all appear here because they determine what counts as the real problem.

Diverge

Exploring alternative paths

Prototype branching and parameter sweeps are used when the risk is narrowing too quickly around the first mathematically convenient option.

Converge

Selecting under evidence

Cross-section tradeoff iteration and exact-versus-bound benchmarking show how I try to justify closure rather than merely declare it.

Represent

Making the work legible

Simulation dashboards, engineering drawings, and decision-support figures are treated as design tools because they changed what the team could see and decide.
