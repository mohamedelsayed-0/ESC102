Praxis I Concepts

Backpack strap project

Concept

Framing

Used in Praxis I

Frame

What I did

I helped widen the problem from "loose straps are annoying" to a fuller set of consequences: snagging, comfort, access to the bag, adjustability, and manufacturability.

Evidence

The evidence sheet and later simulation parameters both reflect that wider frame, because the criteria had to exist before the model meant anything.

Why it mattered

This prevented the project from collapsing into a superficial fastening fix. It also slowed down my habit of reaching for a model before the decision criteria were visible.

What I would watch next time

If the frame narrows too early, later metrics will only optimize one kind of user experience. I would revisit the frame explicitly before each major comparison step.

Concept

Prototyping

Used in Praxis I

Represent

What I did

I supported the team in keeping the elastic, inside-bag adjustment, and shoulder-side adjustment ideas concrete enough to compare instead of letting them stay as loose verbal suggestions.

Evidence

The evidence sheet and MATLAB model both preserve those directions as distinct options with parameters that can be varied and compared.

Why it mattered

Prototyping gave the project something tangible to reason about. It turned discussion from taste and intuition into tradeoffs the team could actually inspect.

What I would watch next time

A prototype only helps if it is tied to the right question. I would pair the early model with faster physical checks so the representation does not carry the full burden by itself.

Concept

Diverging

Used in Praxis I

Diverge

What I did

I helped keep multiple concept directions alive long enough to see what each one solved differently before the team narrowed toward a preferred direction.

Evidence

The project record shows three concept paths, not a single hybrid from the start. That is what made later comparison meaningful.

Why it mattered

Diverging kept the problem open long enough for better distinctions to appear. It also taught me that divergence needs an endpoint, because in this project it ran too long and squeezed convergence time later.

What I would watch next time

I would define what divergence is supposed to uncover before it starts and decide in advance what evidence is enough to begin closing options.

CIV102 Concepts

Bridge project

Concept

Failure-mode framing

Used in CIV102

Frame

What I did

I treated the bridge as a system of distinct failure modes, including tension, compression, buckling, shear, and glue-related failure, rather than one overall strength problem.

Evidence

The hand calculations and analysis code both track separate safety factors across positions on the bridge instead of collapsing the design into one number.

Why it mattered

This made the bridge more legible and showed where geometry decisions actually changed risk. It was also the point where analytical clarity became genuinely useful to the team.

What I would watch next time

Framing by failure mode is incomplete if fabrication quality stays implicit. I would pair it with an equally explicit constructability frame from the start.

Concept

Tradeoff iteration

Used in CIV102

Converge

What I did

I treated bridge geometry as something to iterate, comparing competing effects such as stiffness, buckling resistance, shear flow, glue demand, and diaphragm spacing.

Evidence

The section-property work and the Python envelope analysis show geometry being recalculated and judged through multiple consequences rather than one optimization target.

Why it mattered

Iteration slowed down the temptation to lock onto a single efficient-looking cross-section too early. It forced several capacities to stay visible at the same time.

What I would watch next time

Iteration becomes false rigor if each revision is judged only analytically. I would attach a buildability check to every major geometry change.

Concept

Representing

Used in CIV102

Represent

What I did

I treated the drawings as design work, not as something to produce after the bridge was already decided. They made assumptions about geometry, spacing, and assembly visible to the whole team.

Evidence

The final drawing package translates the analytical bridge into something that could be cut, glued, and assembled from a shared picture.

Why it mattered

This concept corrected a blind spot in my own process. It showed that communication and constructability are not downstream from design; they are part of design.

What I would watch next time

Drawings can still look complete while hiding tolerance risk or assembly difficulty. I would bring them into the iteration earlier, while they still have power to change the design.

Gaussian Concepts

Independent analytic work

Concept

Reduction

Used in Gaussian project

Frame

What I did

I reframed the entanglement problem under a symmetric setting so the threshold condition could be expressed directly in the channel parameters instead of left as repeated numerical evaluation.

Evidence

The long note and benchmark code both center the move from full-spectrum computation toward a reduced, analytically invertible form.

Why it mattered

This created the main leverage in the project. It turned an abstract technical task into a designable representation that could be interpreted and tested.

What I would watch next time

Reduction is powerful only if the assumptions remain visible. I would keep stating what the symmetry buys and what it excludes whenever the result is presented.

Concept

Sweeps

Used in Gaussian project

Diverge

What I did

I explored transmissivity, thermal occupancy, gain, and squeezing across multiple regimes instead of relying on a few convenient examples.

Evidence

The heatmaps and benchmark figures are generated from those sweeps, which make variation itself part of the argument.

Why it mattered

Sweeps widened the project after the abstraction was already in place. They stopped the reduced method from being judged only on the nicest-looking cases.

What I would watch next time

I would keep checking that the sweep ranges are chosen for the real technical question, not just because they produce a cleaner figure.

Concept

Benchmarking

Used in Gaussian project

Converge

What I did

I compared the simplified threshold and bound against exact quantities before treating the reduction as trustworthy enough to guide conclusions.

Evidence

The pure-loss and thermal-loss plots directly compare exact log-negativity and the reference bound, including where visible slack appears.

Why it mattered

Benchmarking is what made the abstraction accountable. It kept a preferred shortcut from being accepted just because it was elegant.

What I would watch next time

Any shortcut that will guide later reasoning needs to be benchmarked in the regime where it will actually be used, not only where it performs best.
