Why These CTMFs Stay

This handbook evaluates practice, not just terminology

I kept only concepts I can defend with project evidence or a credible retrospective reading. Taken together, the set says something specific about my practice rather than just satisfying the course count.

Strength

I become effective when criteria and representations make a decision legible

The strongest entries in this handbook are the ones where the concept created a real comparison, clarified a requirement, or turned an abstract issue into something a team could inspect.

Bias

The set exposes my attraction to tractable structures

The recurring risk is not using concepts at all. It is over-trusting the concepts that create analytical neatness, especially when user reality, fabrication, or exclusions are harder to represent.

Transfer

The future move is earlier checking, not less structure

I am not trying to abandon models or frameworks. I am trying to use them earlier with clearer limits, then bring in verification, build reality, and process correction sooner.

Praxis I Concepts

Backpack strap project

These notes use course concepts to explain how the project moved from a vague complaint about loose straps to a structured comparison between options.

Concept

Design concepts

Used in Praxis I

Diverge

Design concepts mattered because the project needed several real options on the table before any comparison could be meaningful.

What the concept meant here

Here, design concepts meant keeping the elastic strap, inside-bag adjustment, and shoulder-side adjustment ideas as distinct directions instead of collapsing them into a single blended answer too early.

How I applied it

I supported the team in preserving the concept set long enough for each option to be compared against the same measures. The project got stronger because there were actual alternatives to evaluate, not just one preferred direction with minor variations.

Evidence to inspect

The evidence sheet and project summary both show that the work preserved three design concepts rather than presenting one answer from the start.

Project section Evidence sheet PDF

What it helped reveal

Keeping the design concepts distinct made it easier to see that each one solved a different part of the problem. That stopped the team from rewarding the cleanest- looking idea before its tradeoffs were visible.

Limitation

Design concepts only help if they stay tied to a decision. In this project, keeping the concepts open was useful, but it also risked taking too much time away from later convergence.

How I would improve it

I would decide earlier what question each concept is supposed to answer, so the concept set stays open for a reason and then closes on time.

Concept

Evaluation criteria

Used in Praxis I

Frame

Evaluation criteria stopped the project from choosing a concept just because it looked cleaner or more intuitive than the others.

What the concept meant here

Evaluation criteria meant making the judgement standards explicit before treating the model output as meaningful. In this project that included snagging, comfort, access, adjustability, and manufacturability.

How I applied it

I helped convert the project into an explicit comparison problem by tying the concept directions to measurable criteria and then using those criteria to shape what the simulation was actually supposed to report.

Evidence to inspect

The clearest evidence is the shift from requirement notes to explicit comparison metrics in the submitted document and the supporting model.

Project section Submitted PDF MATLAB model

What it helped reveal

Evaluation criteria made it visible that a concept could improve one part of the problem while worsening another. That gave the later comparison real authority instead of letting the project collapse into taste or convenience.

Limitation

The criteria still leaned toward what could be parameterized easily. Quick-use behaviour and physical handling mattered, but they were not represented as strongly as the modeled quantities.

How I would improve it

I would revisit the criteria after the first round of comparison and add a faster physical-use check so the standards are not shaped mostly by the model.

Concept

Proxy testing

Used in Praxis I

Represent

Proxy testing mattered because the team needed a low-resource way to compare concepts before any full ergonomic test was available.

What the concept meant here

Proxy testing means approximating a fuller test with the resources you actually have. In this project, the simplified simulation acted as a proxy for more complete ergonomic evaluation.

How I applied it

I built a simulation-based comparison tool that approximated how the concept directions might shift load distance and pressure. It did not replace real use, but it gave the team a disciplined stand-in when direct testing was limited.

Evidence to inspect

The submitted simulation page and MATLAB source show the proxy test being used to generate information about the concept directions.

Project section Submitted PDF MATLAB model

What it helped reveal

The proxy test gave the project a way to compare concepts without pretending the results were exact. It turned the simulation into an information-generating tool rather than a claim that the body had been modeled perfectly.

Limitation

A proxy test can easily look more authoritative than it deserves. The simplified model still depended on assumptions that were looser than a full ergonomic test would allow.

How I would improve it

I would pair the proxy test with a small physical-use check earlier, so the stand-in stays honest about what it can and cannot claim.

CIV102 Concepts

Bridge project

These entries show where the bridge stopped being a clean analytical exercise and became a design problem tied to course requirements, verification work, and graphical communication.

Concept

Requirements

Used in CIV102

Frame

Requirements made the bridge legible. The project became useful once the span, load, material, and fabrication limits were treated as active design requirements instead of background conditions.

What the concept meant here

Requirements are the conditions the design has to satisfy. In this project that meant the moving train load, geometry limits, matboard constraints, and the realities of how the bridge would actually be assembled.

How I applied it

I treated the bridge geometry as something that had to answer multiple requirements at once, not just maximize one strength measure. That forced the calculations, drawings, and material decisions to stay connected.

Evidence to inspect

The report, calculations, and drawing package all show the bridge being checked against a set of course constraints rather than a single idea of strength.

Project section Design report Calculation package

What it helped reveal

Thinking in terms of requirements made it obvious that no single efficient-looking section would satisfy every demand. The bridge had to balance capacity, buildability, and assembly logic together.

Limitation

Some requirements entered the project later than they should have, especially the constructability and tolerance side. That meant early analytical choices looked better than they really were.

How I would improve it

I would write the build-related requirements down earlier and keep them beside the analytical ones from the start, so convergence cannot happen around strength alone.

Concept

Verification

Used in CIV102

Converge

Verification kept the bridge decisions accountable. The calculations, code, and drawings only mattered because they checked whether the design assumptions were actually holding together.

What the concept meant here

Verification means checking whether the design or argument is holding up against the standards you set for it. Here it included safety-factor checks, moving-load envelopes, and drawing-level confirmation of geometry and assembly assumptions.

How I applied it

I used calculations and code to verify capacity across the bridge and the drawings to verify that the analytical bridge could still be built the way the equations assumed.

Evidence to inspect

The bridge analysis script, calculation package, and drawings all act as verification artifacts in slightly different ways.

Project section Calculation package Analysis code Engineering drawings

What it helped reveal

Verification showed where an analytically efficient bridge could still fail its own assumptions once splice placement, glue, or assembly logic were considered. It kept the project from accepting neat math too quickly.

Limitation

Most of the verification still happened before fabrication. That meant some of the strongest checks were analytical rather than physical, which leaves a gap between proof on paper and proof in the build.

How I would improve it

I would add a faster build-stage verification pass so the bridge is checked against fabrication friction earlier, not only against the calculations.

Concept

Prototypes: graphical

Used in CIV102

Represent

The graphical prototype mattered because the bridge was never complete as an analysis alone. The drawings were where geometry, spacing, and assembly logic became visible enough to challenge.

What the concept meant here

A graphical prototype is a representation used to generate or communicate information about a design concept. In this project, the drawing package did both: it communicated the bridge and exposed what the analysis was assuming.

How I applied it

I treated the drawings as a working prototype of the bridge geometry, not as downstream documentation. They were a way to inspect spacing, assembly sequence, and the placement of critical features before fabrication.

Evidence to inspect

The engineering drawings are the clearest evidence because they translate the design into something other teammates could actually build from.

Project section Engineering drawings

What it helped reveal

The graphical prototype exposed where the bridge was relying on idealized geometry. Once the design had to be drawn for actual construction, tolerance, sequence, and accessibility stopped being background issues.

Limitation

A graphical prototype can still look more complete than the build reality. Clean lines on the page are not yet proof that hand assembly will be easy.

How I would improve it

I would bring the drawings into the project earlier and annotate them more aggressively with tolerance and assembly notes, so the graphical prototype starts changing the design sooner.

Gaussian Concepts

Independent analytic work

These notes focus on course concepts that helped me treat the Gaussian work as an argument rather than only a derivation.

Concept

Analytical

Used in Gaussian project

Frame

The main claim in this project was analytical: it aimed to establish a threshold relation directly from the mathematical structure of the problem.

What the concept meant here

An analytical claim is one that is supported through reasoning about structure, relations, and derivation. In this project the core claim was that the threshold could be written explicitly in the channel parameters.

How I applied it

I reorganized the problem so the partially transposed eigenvalue condition became an explicit inequality rather than a repeated numerical check. That made the main result an analytical claim, not just a computational observation.

Evidence to inspect

The long note and threshold functions in the benchmark script show the analytical claim being made directly at the parameter level.

Project section Long note PDF Benchmark script

What it helped reveal

Treating the result as an analytical claim created direct threshold statements instead of leaving the work at the level of repeated computation. That is what gave the project design leverage.

Limitation

Analytical neatness can make a result look more universal than it is. In this case the claim still depends on the symmetric setting, so its scope has to be stated clearly.

How I would improve it

I would state the boundary of the analytical claim more aggressively and put its limits beside the result every time it is presented.

Concept

Toulman’s structure of argument

Used in Gaussian project

Represent

Toulman’s structure of argument mattered because the project was only convincing once the claim, the grounds behind it, and the warrant connecting them were visible together.

What the concept meant here

Toulman’s structure of argument asks whether a claim is supported by clear grounds and a visible warrant. In this project, the reduced threshold was the claim, the exact-versus- bound figures were part of the grounds, and the warrant was the reasoning for why those comparisons justified using the simpler result.

How I applied it

I used the figures and accompanying prose to make the argument legible instead of asking the reader to accept the reduced formula on elegance alone. The analytical claim had to be carried by explicit support.

Evidence to inspect

The long note and benchmark figures together show how the claim is paired with supporting grounds rather than left as a standalone derivation.

Project section Long note PDF Benchmark script

What it helped reveal

This structure kept the result from resting on elegance alone. It made me ask what evidence the claim actually had, and whether the reader could see why that evidence should count.

Limitation

The warrant can still stay too implicit. A reader can see the plots and still not be told clearly enough why they justify trusting the reduced result in one regime and not another.

How I would improve it

I would label the claim, grounds, and warrant more explicitly in the note and captions, and I would state the rebuttal conditions more directly.

Concept

PIAA

Used in Gaussian project

Converge

PIAA mattered because the figures only became useful once I treated them as a cycle: perceive, interpret, assess, then act on what the evidence was saying.

What the concept meant here

PIAA stands for perceive, interpret, assess, act. In this project it describes how I had to read the plots: first notice what they showed, then interpret the pattern, assess whether the result was still useful, and finally act by tightening or revising the claim.

How I applied it

I used the exact-versus-bound figures and heatmaps as prompts for this sequence. The figures were not just decorative outputs; they changed what I was willing to say about the reduced condition.

Evidence to inspect

The pure-loss plot, the heatmap, and the script that produced them are the clearest evidence because they show the information that had to be perceived and interpreted before any claim could close.

Project section Benchmark script

What it helped reveal

PIAA made the figures part of decision-making. It turned approximation error into something that had to change the argument, not just sit beside it as an interesting visual.

Limitation

If the assessment threshold stays implicit, PIAA can collapse back into intuition. It is easy to look at a plot, feel reassured, and never say exactly what size of gap would change the claim.

How I would improve it

I would write the assessment rule down more clearly in advance, so the final act is tied to a stated decision threshold instead of a general sense that the plot looks good enough.
